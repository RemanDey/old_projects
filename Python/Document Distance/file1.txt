Once upon a time, in a vibrant green forest, lived a boastful Hare who constantly bragged about his speed. He loved to tease a slow-moving Tortoise, often challenging him to races. One sunny morning, the Tortoise, tired of the Hare’s taunts, accepted a challenge. Very well, he said calmly, let's race to the big oak tree on the hill. The other forest animals gathered to watch. The race began, and the Hare shot off like an arrow, leaving the Tortoise far behind in a cloud of dust. "This is too easy!" the Hare thought, laughing to himself. He was so far ahead that he decided to take a short nap under a shady tree, confident he had plenty of time to spare. Meanwhile, the steadfast Tortoise continued to plod along, one slow step after another, never stopping, never resting. He knew he couldn't match the Hare's speed, but he could certainly match his persistence. The Hare awoke with a start. He stretched and yawned, then looked towards the finish line. To his utter shock, he saw the Tortoise just a few feet from the massive oak tree, slowly but surely crossing the finish line. The Hare sprinted with all his might, but it was too late. The Tortoise had already won.
